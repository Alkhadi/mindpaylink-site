Quick Starter: Autism

Educational information only ‚Äî not a diagnosis or medical advice.

What it is

A neurodevelopmental difference that affects social communication, sensory processing and patterns of behaviour or interests.

Possible signs

  • Differences in social communication or interaction
  • Sensory sensitivities (noise, light, textures)
  • Preference for routines; difficulty with change
  • Focused or intense interests

Trusted resources

  • NHS ‚Äì Autism overview

Use this site for Autism

  • Choose a breathing pace that feels safe for your sensory profile.
  • Use predictable counts (e.g., Coherent 5-5) and minimise audio.
  • Save a one-page PDF and share with carers/teachers if helpful.

Evidence snapshot (UK)

Children/young people: structured, communication‚Äëfocused interventions, environmental adaptations and co‚Äëoccurring problem management; consider melatonin via specialists if behavioural sleep approaches fail. Sources: NICE CG170 (support & management). Educational only; not medical advice.

Adults: adapted CBT for anxiety/depression, structured support plans, and workplace/healthcare reasonable adjustments. Sources: NICE autism guidance (adults). Educational only.

Children & Adolescents

  • Naturalistic, caregiver‚Äëmediated coaching (NDBI style): 3‚Äì5 min play bursts, copy/expand/celebrate, many times daily.
  • AAC is speech‚Äëfriendly: pictures/PECS/core boards/apps reduce frustration; keep AAC available everywhere.
  • Visual & structured teaching: First/Then, visual schedules, clear work systems; teach in small steps.
  • Behaviour ‚Üí ABC: track antecedent‚Äìbehaviour‚Äìconsequence; teach an easier way to ask; reinforce quickly.
  • Sleep: regularity, routine, light/noise/temperature; discuss melatonin only with a clinician if insomnia persists.

Adults

  • Employment: reasonable adjustments (Equality Act), clear written instructions, low‚Äësensory spaces, flexible hours/hybrid.
  • Access to Work: workplace assessment, job coach, assistive tech, travel support.
  • Mental health: adapted CBT (visual structure, concrete goals, graded exposure with sensory planning); monitor medication with clinicians.
  • Regulation: micro‚Äëbreaks, noise management, meltdown/shutdown plans.

Later Life

  • Anticipate sensory/hearing/vision changes; schedule quieter appointments.
  • Use pill organisers, checklists, and carer prompts; prepare hospital passports.
  • Ask the local authority about adult social care and carers‚Äô assessments.

Neurodevelopmental: ADHD (quick pointers)

  • Core features: inattention, impulsivity, hyperactivity across settings.
  • Supports: behavioural strategies, executive‚Äëfunction scaffolds, environmental cues; review sleep, anxiety.

Mental Health: Anxiety ‚Ä¢ Depression ‚Ä¢ Stress ‚Ä¢ Sleep

  • CBT (adapted): visual agendas, concrete skills practice, stepwise exposure with sensory planning.
  • Sleep: consistent timing, low light, screens off ‚â•1h; record latency; escalate to clinician if persistent.

Breathing & Focus (2‚Äëminute reset)

  1. Sit comfortably; relax shoulders and jaw.
  2. Inhale quietly through the nose ~4 s; exhale ~6 s.
  3. Repeat 10 cycles; notice shoulders lowering and heart rate easing.

Use before transitions, meetings or bedtime wind‚Äëdown.

Practical, evidence‚Äëinformed techniques

      
    
        
  • NDBI‚Äëstyle caregiver coaching (ages 2‚Äì8): brief play bursts with modelling and turn‚Äëtaking to grow
          communication.
    • 
        
  • Visual schedules and First/Then (ages 3‚Äì16): make steps visible; reduce uncertainty and transitions stress.
        
    • 
        
  • AAC basics (all ages): core boards or PECS to reduce frustration; AAC supports, not replaces, speech.
    • 
        
  • Structured teaching/TEACCH (school age): clear work systems, chunked tasks and predictable routines.
    • 
        
  • ABC behaviour logging (all ages): track antecedents and teach easier, functional communication alternatives.
        
    • 
        
  • Sleep routines (all ages): consistent schedule, dim light, calming steps; seek specialist advice for
          melatonin.
    • 
        
  • Sensory regulation menu (all ages): headphones, movement breaks, deep‚Äëpressure tools if liked; stop if
          distress.
    • 
        
  • Adapted CBT (adolescents/adults): concrete goals, visual agendas, graded exposure with sensory planning.
        
    • 
        
  • Reasonable adjustments (adolescents/adults): clear written instructions, reduced sensory load, flexible
          timings.
